animals are kept within enclosures for public exhibition and often bred for conservation purposes

type of ZOOs

Safari park
- a parklike zoo in which wild animals are allowed to roam free in an environment designed
to resemble their natural habitat and are observed by visitors riding through the park in cars or buses
Aquaria
- A public aquarium (plural: public aquaria) or public water zoo is the aquatic counterpart of a zoo,
which houses living aquatic animal and plant specimens for public viewing.
Most public aquariums feature tanks larger than those kept by home aquarists,
as well as smaller tanks.
Roadside zoos
- Small, unaccredited zoos where wild and exotic animals suffer in captivity.
Roadside zoos dot the American landscape.
They're generally small menageries where wild animals like lions, tigers, monkeys, wolves, and others are kept in captivity, and often suffer badly
Petting zoos
- A petting zoo (also called a children's zoo, children's farm, or petting farm) features a combination of domesticated animals and some wild species that are docile enough to touch
and feed. In addition to independent petting zoos, many general zoos contain a petting zoo.
Animal theme parks
- An animal theme park, also known as a zoological theme park, is a combination of an amusement park and a zoo,
mainly for entertainment, amusement, and commercial purposes.
